VENUS

Planet Venus

Venus, often called Earth's "sister planet," is similar in size and structure but has extreme surface conditions. Its thick atmosphere, mainly composed of carbon dioxide, traps heat, making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system. Despite its surface being volcanic and rocky, it is largely uninhabitable due to its intense heat and pressure.

Facts About Venus

  • Diameter: 7,520 miles (12,104 km)
  • Circumference: 23,600 miles (37,400 km)
  • Surface Area: 177.7 million square miles (460 million km²)
  • Volume: 0.9 times that of Earth
  • Mass: 4.87 × 10²⁴ kg
  • Distance from Sun: 67 million miles (108 million km)
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 243 Earth days (retrograde rotation)
  • Tilt of Axis: 177.4°
  • Atmosphere Composition: Mainly carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid

Potential for Life on Venus

  • Atmosphere: Venus has a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide, making life impossible.
  • Surface: Surface temperature reaches 900°F (475°C), too hot for life to exist.
  • Moons: Venus has no moons, and no signs of life have been discovered through exploration.
  • Harsh Conditions: Intense pressure and constant volcanic activity make Venus uninhabitable.

Venus's Surface and Atmosphere

  • Thick Atmosphere: Venus has a dense atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, causing a strong greenhouse effect.
  • Surface Temperature: With average temperatures of 900°F (475°C), Venus is hotter than Mercury, despite being farther from the Sun.
  • Volcanic Activity: Venus is believed to have many volcanoes, some possibly still active, contributing to its extreme surface conditions.
