NEPTUNE

Planet Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. Famous for its deep blue hue, it is a gas giant with incredibly strong, supersonic winds and massive storms. Despite receiving little sunlight, Neptune emits more heat than it absorbs, suggesting a mysterious internal heat source.

Facts Of Neptune

  • Diameter: 30,598 miles (49,244 km)
  • Distance from Sun: 2.8 billion miles (4.5 billion km)
  • Orbital Period: 165 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: About 16 hours
  • Atmosphere Composition: Hydrogen, helium, and methane
  • Moons: 14 known moons, including Triton
  • Rings: 5 faint rings made of dust and ice
  • Winds: Some of the fastest in the solar system (up to 1,300 mph)
  • Temperature: Extremely cold with an internal heat source
  • Core: Rocky core surrounded by icy mantle

Winds & Storms

  • Powerful Winds: Neptune experiences the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds up to 1,200 mph (1,930 km/h), slicing through its icy atmosphere.
  • Hurricane-like Storms: Massive storms like the Great Dark Spot resemble Jupiter’s Great Red Spot and are large enough to engulf Earth.
  • Atmospheric Turbulence: The planet's dynamic weather patterns shift rapidly, showing intense cloud movements and unpredictable changes.

Internal Structure & Magnetism

  • Core: Likely composed of rock and ice
  • Mantle: Thick layer of water, ammonia, and methane ices
  • Magnetic Field: Tilted 47 degrees from its rotation axis
