JUPITER

Planet Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the solar system. It's a gas giant with a thick hydrogen-helium atmosphere, famous for the Great Red Spot and its 80+ moons, including Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Facts About Jupiter

  • Diameter: 86,881 miles (139,820 km)
  • Circumference: 273,000 miles (439,264 km)
  • Surface Area: 24.7 billion square miles (64.5 billion km²)
  • Volume: 1,321 times that of Earth
  • Mass: 1.898 × 10²⁷ kg
  • Distance from Sun: 484 million miles (778 million km)
  • Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 9 hours, 56 minutes
  • Tilt of Axis: 3.1°
  • Atmosphere Composition: Mainly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and water vapor

Jupiter's Surface and Atmosphere

  • Atmosphere: Jupiter has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with clouds of ammonia, methane, and water vapor.
  • Moons: Jupiter has 80 known moons. The largest are Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto, also known as the Galilean moons.
