URANUS

Planet Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, has a distinct blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side with an axial tilt of nearly 98 degrees, causing extreme seasonal variations. Its atmosphere is mainly composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, with visible cloud bands and storms.

Facts About Uranus

  • Diameter: 31,518 miles (50,724 km)
  • Circumference: 99,000 miles (159,000 km)
  • Surface Area: 3.1 billion square miles (8.1 billion km²)
  • Volume: 63 times that of Earth
  • Mass: 8.68 × 10²⁴ kg
  • Distance from Sun: 1.9 billion miles (3 billion km)
  • Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 17.2 hours
  • Tilt of Axis: 97.77°
  • Atmosphere Composition: Hydrogen, helium, and methane

Potential for Life on Uranus

  • Atmosphere Composition: Mainly hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a blue-green hue.
  • Extreme Tilt: Uranus has an extreme tilt, rotating on its side with its poles facing the Sun.
  • Storms: Though less active than Neptune, Uranus has wind speeds up to 560 mph (900 km/h).
