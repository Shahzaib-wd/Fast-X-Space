Evolution of Earth

From Fiery Beginnings to a Life-Supporting Planet

1. Formation

The Earth formed around 4.5 billion years ago from the dust and gas left over after the formation of the Sun. Through a process of accretion and collisions with other bodies, it eventually became a molten sphere.

2. Cooling & Crust Formation

As Earth cooled, a solid crust formed over the surface. Volcanic activity was intense, and the atmosphere began to take shape from released gases, mainly carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and water vapor.

3. Oceans & First Life

4. Oxygenation & Evolution

Cyanobacteria began photosynthesizing, releasing oxygen. This led to the Great Oxygenation Event, drastically changing Earth's atmosphere. Complex life evolved, leading to plants, animals, and eventually humans.
