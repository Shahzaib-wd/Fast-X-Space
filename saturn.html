SATURN

Planet Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system. Known for its stunning ring system, Saturn is a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Despite its size, it has a low density and could float in water if a large enough body existed.

Facts About Saturn

  • Diameter: 74,900 miles (120,500 km)
  • Rings: Made of ice and rock, the most prominent in the solar system
  • Moons: Over 145 known moons, including Titan and Enceladus
  • Atmosphere: Composed mainly of hydrogen and helium
  • Distance from Sun: 886 million miles (1.4 billion km)
  • Orbital Period: 29.5 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: About 10.7 hours
  • Temperature: Averages -288°F (-178°C)
  • Storms: Features massive storms, including the famous hexagon at the north pole
  • Magnetic Field: Strong and symmetrical, unlike most other planets

Unique Features

  • Rings: Saturn’s rings are the largest, brightest, and most complex in the solar system, made mostly of ice particles and rock.
  • Hexagon Storm: A massive, six-sided jet stream at Saturn's north pole, spanning over 30,000 km and persisting for decades.
  • Titan: Saturn’s largest moon, Titan, has a thick nitrogen-rich atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane on its surface.

Atmosphere & Weather

  • Storms: Saturn has violent storms and wind speeds of up to 1,800 km/h.
  • Temperature: Extremely cold, averaging -178°C (-288°F).
  • Composition: Mostly hydrogen and helium with bands of clouds.
