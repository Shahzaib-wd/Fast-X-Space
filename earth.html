EARTH

Planet Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. It is a dynamic and diverse world, characterized by its unique combination of atmosphere, water, and geological activity.

Facts Of Earth

  • Diameter: 7,926 miles (12,760 km)
  • Circumference: 24,901 miles (40,075 km)
  • Surface Area: 196.9 million square miles (510 million km²)
  • Volume: 261 billion cubic miles (1.083 × 10¹² km³)
  • Mass: 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg
  • Distance from Sun: 93 million miles (150 million km)
  • Orbital Period: 365.25 days
  • Orbit:Elliptical path around the Sun, with varying distances affecting seasonal changes
  • Rotation Period: 23 hours, 56 minutes
  • Tilt of Axis: 23.4° (responsible for seasons)
  • Atmosphere Composition: 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 1% other gases

Life on Earth

  • Biodiversity: Home to millions of species, including plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms.
  • Habitability: Conditions such as liquid water, suitable temperatures, and an atmosphere make Earth unique in supporting life.
  • Ecosystems: Forests, oceans, deserts, and wetlands, each with distinct communities of organisms.

Surface and Water

  • Oceans: Cover approximately 70% of Earth's surface, containing about 97% of its water.
  • Freshwater: Only about 3% of Earth's water is freshwater, with two-thirds locked in glaciers and polar ice caps.
  • Landforms: Includes mountains, valleys, plains, deserts, and forests.
