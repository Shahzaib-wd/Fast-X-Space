Big Bang Theory

The origin of everything, explained by science

What is the Big Bang Theory?

The Big Bang Theory is the prevailing cosmological model explaining the origin of the universe. It proposes that the universe began as a singular, infinitely hot and dense point approximately 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since.

Historical Background

Discovered by: Georges Lemaître (1927)
Supported by: Edwin Hubble's redshift observations
Confirmed by: Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation discovery in 1965

The theory gained traction after Hubble's law demonstrated galaxies were moving away, indicating expansion. The CMB discovery solidified it as the leading explanation of the universe's birth.

Scientific Significance

  • Explains cosmic microwave background radiation
  • Foundation for modern cosmology and astrophysics
  • Predicts nucleosynthesis and element formation
  • Links with General Relativity and quantum mechanics

Recent Discoveries & Research

In 2024, James Webb Space Telescope captured early galaxy formations supporting Big Bang timelines. Ongoing research aims to understand the moments after inflation and test multiverse theories.
