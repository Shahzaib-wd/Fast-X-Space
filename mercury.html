MERCURY

Planet Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest one in our solar system. It has no atmosphere to retain heat, causing extreme temperature variations. Its surface is covered with craters and ancient lava plains, resembling Earth's Moon.

Facts About Mercury

  • Diameter: 4,879 km (3,032 miles)
  • Surface Area: 74.8 million km²
  • Mass: 3.3 × 10²³ kg
  • Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 59 Earth days
  • Temperature Range: -173°C to 427°C
  • Moons: None
  • Surface: Rocky, cratered, with cliffs and ridges
  • Magnetic Field: Weak but present

Surface And Temperature

  • Surface: Mercury’s surface is similar to the Moon’s, marked by countless impact craters and vast lava plains formed by ancient volcanic activity.
  • Atmosphere: It has an extremely thin atmosphere, known as an exosphere, mostly composed of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium.
  • Tectonic Activity: Mercury features enormous cliff-like formations called scarps, which were created as the planet cooled and contracted over time.

Ice At The Poles

  • Surprising Fact: Despite being close to the Sun, Mercury has water ice.
  • Polar Craters: Permanently shadowed regions may contain frozen deposits.
  • Temperature Protection: These craters are never hit by sunlight.
